Responsible Disclosure

Security Policy

We value the security of our users and data. If you believe you’ve found a vulnerability, please tell us so we can fix it quickly.

Scope

This policy applies to the properties we control: pricingforprofit.co and any subdomains hosted by us on Netlify.

How to Report

Email: mikeguidesblog@gmail.com

Security TXT: /.well-known/security.txt

Please include a clear description, steps to reproduce, the affected URL(s), and any proof-of-concept code or screenshots.

Safe Harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

  • Act in good faith and follow this policy.
  • Avoid privacy violations, data destruction, or service disruption.
  • Limit access to what’s necessary to demonstrate the issue.
  • Give us a reasonable time to fix the issue before public disclosure.

Rules of Engagement

  • No DDoS, spam, or brute force attacks.
  • No social engineering of our team or service providers.
  • No access to, or exfiltration of, other users’ personal data.
  • Use your own accounts for testing where applicable.
  • Automated scanners are fine at a low rate; don’t degrade availability.

Out of Scope

  • Reports about third-party services we don’t control.
  • Best-practice advisories without a proof-of-impact (e.g., missing CSP, cookie flags on non-auth pages).
  • Clickjacking on pages with no sensitive actions.
  • Rate limiting on non-auth endpoints.

Triage & Response

We aim to:

  • Acknowledge your report within 3 business days.
  • Keep you updated at key points (reproduced, fix in progress, fix deployed).
  • Remediate valid issues as quickly as practical, typically within 30 days depending on severity.

We currently do not offer monetary bounties. With your permission, we may credit you on a future Hall of Fame page.

Coordinated Disclosure: Please refrain from public disclosure until a fix is released. We’ll coordinate timelines with you in good faith.
